My name is Adrienne Williams and I am nineteen years old. Currently I attend the University of California Riverside and I am a first year Theater, Film, Digital Production major (TFDP) under the College of Humanities and Social Science. My whole life I have been passionate about story telling and being able to introduce people to diverse perspectives. Growing up I was always a creative mind, easily gravitating towards the arts from a young age. I got into painting and photography in middle school and it made me realize the power that visual story telling holds. Although I found myself able to impact a small audience with my art works, I always wanted to do more. I wanted my stories to transcend the 2D world, I wanted them to transcend language. Photography was a great passion of mine and this has translated into a broader interest in digital production and film. Throughout high school I began studying film and digital production including many forms of digital storytelling. With my degree, I hope to change the way that stories are told within the film industry in order to make space for diverse perspectives. I'd like to control the narrative, to be able to show people things they've never seen before. I'd love to see more representations of people like myself because I find that representation fosters empathy and fights prejudice. Eventually, I'd like to open my own digital production studio to help other passionate story tellers tell tales of their own.
